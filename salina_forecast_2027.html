⚡ ENSO context — El Niño 2026 → 2027

  • Dry season 2027 (Jan–Apr) longer and hotter: harvest window extended 10–14 extra days vs. average.

  • Temperatures 1–2°C above average during dry months: accelerated evaporation, favorable for brine concentration.
  • Eastern Pacific cyclone activity reduced under El Niño years (historically lower formation rates).

El Niño 2026–2027 — impact on the salt operation

What does El Niño mean for Guatemala’s Pacific coast?

El Niño warms the equatorial Pacific, weakening the trade winds and reducing the moisture that normally drives rainfall on Guatemala’s Pacific slope. INSIVUMEH consistently documents 15–30% rainfall deficits on the Pacific coast during moderate El Niño events, with the mid-summer dry spell extending to 8–10 weeks (vs. the normal 4–6 weeks).

  • Pacific SST off Chiquimulilla: rises ~1.3–1.4°C above normal at event peak (Dec–Mar).
  • 2027 rain onset: may be delayed 2–3 weeks vs. average (normally May), extending the dry season.
  • 2027 rain season end: possibly early October, shortening the late rainy season.
  • Second-half rainfall: more intense and concentrated in short burst events (risk of pond damage from high 24h accumulations).
